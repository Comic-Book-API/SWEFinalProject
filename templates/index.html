The History

The history of Marvel started in 1939 by Martin Goodman undernaeth a number of imprints, which is known as Timely Comics. Timely's first publication, Marvel Comics #1, included the first superhero the Human Torch. After a while, Timely changed its name to Atlas Comics in 1951, and eventually became Marvel Comics in the early 1960s. Atlas Comics expanded into a wider variety of genres, featuring horror, Westerns, humor, crime, war comics, etc.. In 1961, Stan Lee revolutionized superhero comics by introducing superheroes designed to appeal to an older audience than the predominantly child audiences of the medium, which what Marvel later called the Marvel Age of Comics. The company launched The Fantastic Four and other comics that was created by Stan Lee, Jack Kirby, Steve Dikto and many others. Following The Fantastic Four, Marvel comics developed a reputation for focusing on characterization and adult issues to a greater extent.
